- (direction by user) User chose Creative Partner mode.
- (idea by user) Build a utility website where people upload PDF bank statements and convert them to Excel.
- (question by user) Explore monetization with Google AdSense.
- (insight by coach) AdSense appears possible in principle, but the product must be designed around privacy, no PII passed to Google, consent/disclosures, and careful ad placement.
- (technique) started Trust-First MVP Lens.
- (direction by coach) Use a facilitator-chosen batch: Trust-First MVP Lens, Revenue Ladder, Differentiation Wedge.
- (idea by coach) Make the first promise "convert locally or delete immediately" rather than "free PDF converter."
- (idea by coach) Add a visible privacy receipt after conversion: file deleted, no account required, no transaction data stored.
- (idea by coach) Offer sample statement demo mode so users can test accuracy before uploading real bank data.
- (direction by user) Target freelancers, small business owners, accountants/bookkeepers, and ordinary people.
- (insight by user) User likes the privacy angle.
- (idea by coach) Build one core converter with audience-specific entry pages for freelancers, small businesses, bookkeepers, and personal finance users.
- (technique) started Revenue Ladder.
- (direction by user) Prefer no paid version; monetize with AdSense.
- (insight by coach) AdSense-only favors high organic traffic, repeat SEO pages, and low support cost; bank statement conversion may be trust-sensitive and conversion sessions may be short.
- (direction by user) User wants a bare utility tool, not a broader finance-document toolkit.
- (idea by coach) Keep the converter page sparse: headline, privacy promise, upload box, output format choice, conversion status, download, and one or two restrained ad placements outside the sensitive flow.
- (direction by user) Converter should handle both digital PDFs and scanned/image-based bank statements with OCR.
- (insight by coach) Supporting scanned statements increases usefulness but makes OCR accuracy, confidence scoring, and privacy architecture more important.
- (question by user) Can the product balance fast and accurate rather than choosing one.
- (insight by coach) Balance speed and accuracy by making quick conversion the default while surfacing confidence, preview, and optional review controls for risky rows.
- (decision by user) Use hybrid processing: browser/local extraction for digital PDFs, server-side OCR for scanned statements.
- (decision by user) Inform users whenever server-side processing is required.
- (idea by coach) Make the processing mode visible: "Processed in your browser" for digital PDFs and "OCR requires secure server processing" for scanned PDFs.
- (direction by user) Support all statement types, including bank and credit card statements.
- (insight by coach) Position as a financial statement converter rather than only a bank statement converter, while keeping bank-statement SEO pages later if needed.
- (decision by user) Make the conversion preview editable before download.
- (idea by coach) Keep editing lightweight: editable cells, add/delete row, column mapping, undo, and confidence highlights for OCR issues.
- (decision by user) Support password-protected PDFs when the user provides the password.
- (decision by user) Clearly inform users that the PDF password is not stored anywhere.
- (idea by coach) Unlock password-protected digital PDFs in-browser when possible; if scanned OCR needs server processing, ask explicit consent and state that the password is used only to open the file for this conversion.
